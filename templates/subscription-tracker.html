The problem

Subscription billing is designed to be invisible

Subscription billing is designed to be invisible. Small amounts, charged to a card you don't check closely, under company names that don't always match the service you remember signing up for.

A free trial that converted to paid without a clear reminder. An annual renewal you forgot about until the charge appeared. A service you used for three months and intended to cancel but didn't.

The average UK household spends significantly more on subscriptions than they think they do — and a meaningful portion of that spend is on services they rarely or never use.

How it works

Step by step

  1. Download the Excel file and open the Tracker tab.
  2. List every subscription you can find — check your bank statement for recurring charges, your phone's App Store subscriptions list, and your email for renewal receipts.
  3. For each subscription, fill in the cost, billing date, and how often you actually use it.
  4. The Annual Cost Calculator automatically totals your monthly and annual spend, calculates your ghost spend (subscriptions rated Rarely or Never), and shows it as a percentage.
  5. The Cancellation Action List pulls every subscription you've marked as Cancel or Review into one place — your to-do list for this month.
Who it's for

Anyone with a bank account and more than three subscriptions

Which is most people. The tracker is deliberately simple — list, rate, review. No technical knowledge needed.
