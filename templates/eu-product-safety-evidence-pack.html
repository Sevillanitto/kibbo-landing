The problem

A product-safety report is only as strong as the details you can name

The EU Safety Gate (formerly RAPEX) is a genuinely powerful system — a single consumer report can trigger an EU-wide alert, force a recall, and get dangerous goods pulled from shelves across 30 countries. But it runs on specifics: the exact brand and model, the batch or lot number, the barcode, where and when you bought it, and clear photos of the hazard. Vague reports stall; precise ones move.

Those details are easy to lose — the packaging gets binned, the receipt fades, the batch number rubs off. Capturing them in one structured pack the moment you spot a hazard is what turns "this seems dangerous" into an actionable report the authorities can trace to a specific production run.

How it works

Step by step

  1. Download the PDF pack and the Excel version the moment you notice a hazard or defect.
  2. Record the brand, model, and the batch/lot number and barcode — the identifiers that tie the item to a specific production run.
  3. Log where you bought it and the date of purchase, and write a clear description of the hazard or defect.
  4. Work through the photos checklist so nothing essential is missed — the item, the label, the batch code, and the hazard itself.
  5. Submit your report through the EU Safety Gate portal, then log the Safety Gate reference number in the pack so you can track it.
Who it's for

Anyone reporting a dangerous product in the EU/EEA

A toy, appliance, cosmetic, or gadget that's unsafe — a complete, dated evidence pack is what lets an authority trace it to a production run and act across the whole single market.
