Legal & Contracts

Small claims, free contract generators and solicitor finders.

← All free resources

Most legal problems consumers face don't need a solicitor. Small claims court, free contract generators, and legal advice charities handle the vast majority of disputes at no cost. These are the official, free starting points before you ever pay for legal help.

Need a document fast? Our free legal letter generator and templates cover the most common consumer disputes.

Take a company to small claims court in the UK without a solicitor

Gov.uk — Make a Court Claim (Small Claims, UK) gov.uk/make-court-claim-for-money →

What: The official online portal to start a small claims court case in England and Wales. Claims up to £10,000 cost £35–£410 to file and don't require a solicitor.

When to use: When a company owes you money and has ignored all other attempts to resolve it. Most companies settle before the hearing.

Get free legal advice on your consumer rights (UK)

Citizens Advice — Legal Help citizensadvice.org.uk/law-and-courts →

What: Free guidance on legal rights, court processes, and when you might need a solicitor.

When to use: Before starting any legal process — understand your position first.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Get a free legally reviewed freelance contract (US)

Freelancers Union — Free Contract Generator (US) contracts.freelancersunion.org →

What: Free, legally reviewed contract template for freelancers. Covers payment terms, scope, kill fees, and intellectual property.

When to use: Before starting any paid freelance project. A signed contract is your only protection.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Find a regulated solicitor in England and Wales

Law Society — Find a Solicitor (UK) solicitors.lawsociety.org.uk →

What: Official directory of regulated solicitors in England and Wales.

When to use: When you need professional legal advice — many solicitors offer a free initial consultation.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Find a verified, licensed attorney in your state

American Bar Association — Lawyer Referral Directory americanbar.org/groups/legal_services/flras →

What: Directory linking to each state bar's official Lawyer Referral Service, which matches you with a licensed, verified attorney — often with a low-cost initial consultation ($25-$50, sometimes free for certain case types).

When to use: Before hiring any attorney, especially for anything beyond a straightforward small claims case.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Report unpaid wages or overtime to the Department of Labor

US Department of Labor — Wage and Hour Division (WHD) dol.gov/agencies/whd →

What: Free federal investigation of unpaid wages, overtime, and minimum wage violations under the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA). Complaints are confidential.

When to use: If your employer isn't paying you correctly for hours worked, including unpaid overtime or illegal tip deductions.

Check your state's non-compete enforceability rules

FTC — Non-Compete Clause Rule (status page) ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/rules/non-compete-clause-rule →

What: Official FTC page tracking the status of federal non-compete regulation (the 2024 rule was formally withdrawn in February 2026) — useful background before checking your specific state's law.

When to use: Before assuming a signed non-compete agreement is automatically enforceable.
