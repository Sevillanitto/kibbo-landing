Housing & Rentals

Deposit disputes, free legal advice and landlord checks.

← All free resources

Tenancy deposit disputes are one of the most common consumer problems in the UK. These are the official channels — all free, no solicitor needed. If your deposit was protected properly, the scheme that holds it must offer you free dispute resolution.

Need to write to your landlord? Use our free letter generator to draft a deposit dispute letter.

Check if your landlord has legally protected your deposit (UK)

Shelter Tenancy Deposit Checker england.shelter.org.uk/housing_advice/tenancy_deposits →

What: Searches all three government-approved deposit protection schemes (TDS, DPS and MyDeposits) in one place to verify your deposit is legally protected.

When to use: At the start of any tenancy in the UK, or before leaving a property. If your landlord hasn't protected your deposit within 30 days of receiving it, you can claim up to 3x the deposit amount in court.

Dispute unfair deposit deductions for free (UK)

Tenancy Deposit Scheme — Raise a Dispute tenancydepositscheme.com/raising-a-dispute →

What: Free Alternative Dispute Resolution service. You don't need a solicitor — just submit your evidence and the scheme adjudicates.

When to use: When your landlord proposes deposit deductions you consider unfair or exaggerated.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Dispute a deposit held with DPS

Deposit Protection Service depositprotection.com/i-am-a-tenant/dispute-resolution →

What: Free dispute resolution for deposits protected with the DPS scheme.

When to use: If your deposit certificate shows DPS as the scheme and your landlord is withholding unfairly.

Dispute a deposit held with MyDeposits

MyDeposits mydeposits.co.uk/tenants/dispute-resolution →

What: Free dispute resolution for deposits protected with MyDeposits.

When to use: If your deposit certificate shows MyDeposits as the scheme.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Get free emergency housing legal advice (UK)

Shelter England shelter.org.uk →

What: Free housing charity offering legal advice, emergency help, and template letters for tenants facing eviction, disrepair, deposit disputes, or illegal landlord behaviour.

Helpline: 0808 800 4444

When to use: Any housing problem where you need legal guidance quickly — Shelter's advice is equivalent to a solicitor consultation at no cost.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Understand your rights as a tenant before taking action (UK)

Citizens Advice Housing citizensadvice.org.uk/housing →

What: Free, comprehensive guidance on renting rights, eviction processes, repair obligations, and landlord disputes.

When to use: Before contacting your landlord formally — understand exactly what you're entitled to demand and what the landlord is legally required to provide.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Look up your state's security deposit return law (US)

HUD — Tenant Rights, Laws and Protections (by state) hud.gov/topics/rental_assistance/tenantrights →

What: The official HUD hub that links to each state's tenant rights, laws and protections — including the statutory deadline your landlord has to return a security deposit and any bad-faith penalties.

When to use: Before you move out, and again if your landlord misses the deadline — there's no federal deposit law, so the exact number (e.g. California 21 days, New York 14, Texas 30) comes from your state's statute.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Report unsafe rental conditions to code enforcement (US)

USA.gov — Housing Complaints usa.gov/housing-complaints →

What: The official government directory for complaints about bad rental conditions — points you to your local code enforcement or health department, which can inspect and fine a landlord independently of your own claims.

When to use: When a landlord ignores a written repair notice for a habitability issue (no heat, mold, no running water) — an official inspection report is strong, independent evidence in housing court.

Want to know more? Read our full guide →

Get emergency legal help for an illegal lockout (US)

LawHelp.org — Free Legal Aid by State lawhelp.org →

What: Finds free and low-cost legal aid organizations in your state, including tenant and eviction help — the place to turn when a landlord has changed the locks, cut utilities, or removed your belongings without a court order.

When to use: The moment a self-help eviction happens — many housing courts have an expedited emergency process (often heard within 24-48 hours) to restore your access.
