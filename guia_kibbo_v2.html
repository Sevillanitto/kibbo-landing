The problem and the solution

Why Kibbo exists and what market it serves

What are dark patterns and why do they matter?

⚠️ The problem

72% of European e-commerce sites use at least one psychological manipulation technique according to European Commission studies (2022).
False urgency: "Only 2 units left" when there are hundreds in stock. Forces users to buy without thinking.
Pre-checked boxes: insurance, subscriptions, and add-ons that sneak into the cart if the user doesn't spot them in time.
Artificially inflated prices: the crossed-out price was never real. The 40% discount is fictional.
Abusive fine print: automatic renewals, forced arbitration, and impossible cancellations hidden in 40-page T&Cs.
Phishing and fraud: fake SMS from postal services, traffic agencies, and banks trick thousands of people every week.

✅ The solution — Kibbo

Kibbo acts in real time while the user browses, without any action required. Install once and it works on its own.
34 active features cover the entire online shopping cycle: from the product page to checkout and T&Cs.
Compatible with more than 30,000 stores with no configuration. The 50 largest stores in Spain have specific implementation.
No user data. All analysis occurs in the browser. No server, no user database, no tracking.
Operational cost of less than €5/day with 1,000 active users using AI features.
Approved in Chrome Web Store and Microsoft Edge Add-ons Store. Ready for immediate distribution.
Regulatory context

The EU Omnibus Directive (2022) and the Digital Services Act (2023) require European e-commerce to eliminate dark patterns. Fines for non-compliance can reach 4% of global revenue. Kibbo is the tool that puts that power in the hands of the individual consumer.

Operational cost of AI features: Kibbo uses its own proxy hosted on Cloudflare Workers that intermediates with the Anthropic API (Claude Sonnet). The user's browser never contacts Anthropic directly. Estimated cost per call: €0.001–€0.003. With 1,000 active daily users using AI features once a day, the total cost is under €3/day. The remaining 29 features are completely free — local analysis in the browser with no external calls.

Automatic features

Activated without user interaction when entering any compatible website

No interaction

False urgency detector + Launch price alert
Analyzes the visible text of each DOM element against a database of 204 phrases classified into three categories: scarcity ("Only 2 units left"), time-limited ("Offer ends in 2h"), and social pressure ("47 people watching this now"). When it detects a trap phrase, it injects a warning pill next to the element indicating the type of manipulation. Countdown timers are detected via clock-format regex and visually neutralized. Also detects "launch price" and "welcome offer" phrases to alert when the discount is temporary by design.
✓ Zero cost — local analysis
Technical implementation

MutationObserver with subtree:true detects new nodes in real time. Each node passes through an exclusion filter (Kibbo's own elements, sidebar areas, suggested items) before being analyzed. Processed in batches of 40 nodes per tick using requestIdleCallback to avoid blocking the main thread. Phrases dismissed by the user are persisted in chrome.storage.local.

Cookie equalizer
When the page loads, identifies the cookie banner using selectors from the 4 most-used CMPs in Spain (OneTrust, Cookiebot, Axeptio, Tarteaucitron) plus dozens of generic classes and additional CMPs (Usercentrics, Quantcast, Didomi). When it finds the banner, it copies the exact padding, font-size, font-weight, border-radius, and min-width from the "Accept" button and applies them to the "Reject" button, adding Kibbo's cyan color so it stands out visually. A discreet badge is shown for 4 seconds.
✓ Zero cost — local analysis

Psychological pricing badge
Detects prices ending in .99 or .95 on product pages and shows a discreet badge next to the price with the real rounded value. Example: "€32.99" → badge "≈ €33 real". The logic identifies the main price on the page (not prices in sidebars or suggested items) to avoid false positives. Runs with retries at 2, 5, and 9 seconds to cover pages with lazy loading.

Price Spectrometer

Local price history per product with trend analysis

Automatic
📈
Price Spectrometer with sparkline
On each visit to a product page, Kibbo extracts the current price and saves it in chrome.storage.local indexed by URL. When the same product is visited again, it compares against the history and calculates whether the price went up, down, or stayed the same. The popup shows a mini SVG chart (sparkline) with the historical trend, recorded minimum and maximum, and a cursor on the risk bar (Green=low price, Red=high price). If it detects that the crossed-out price doesn't match the real historical price, it shows a "CAUGHT!" banner on the page.
✓ Zero cost — local storage, no backend

Fake discount detector / CAUGHT!
When the spectrometer has a product's history and the visible crossed-out price on the page (the "before sale" price) doesn't match any price recorded in the user's previous visits, it shows a red banner at the top of the page: "CAUGHT! The real discount is X%, not the advertised Y%. Kibbo has no record of this product having cost €Z recently." Requires at least one prior visit to the same product to have comparison data.
✓ Zero cost — local history comparison
How to test it

Checkout protection

Features that act at the moment of payment

Automatic
🔐
Checkout shield
When detecting a checkout page (by URL, DOM selectors, and presence of payment forms), it shows a bar at the top of the browser with a summary of the session's security status: whether the protocol is HTTPS, whether insurance or hidden add-ons were detected on the page, and the overall risk level. The goal is to give the user a "traffic light" before entering their payment data.
✓ Zero cost — local DOM analysis
Where to test it

Add any product to the cart on Amazon, Ryanair, or Zara and proceed to the payment step. The bar appears automatically.

💳
Payment gateway verifier
On checkout pages, analyzes the page HTML looking for signals from known payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, Redsys, Adyen, Braintree, Bizum, Klarna, Sequra). If a recognized gateway is detected, shows a discreet green badge for 8 seconds ("✓ Secure payment · Stripe"). If it finds a form with card fields and no recognized gateway, shows an orange warning "⚠️ Unverified gateway".
✓ Zero cost — local DOM analysis
🛒
Cart guardian
Compares the price the spectrometer saved on the product page with the total visible at checkout. If the total exceeds the remembered price by more than €2 or 5%, it shows a red banner at the top: "The price went up +€X (+Y%) since you viewed the product. Remembered price: €X · Current total: €Y." Detects when stores add extras, insurance, or fees between the product page and the payment step.
✓ Zero cost — local storage comparison
Note: Requires having visited the product page in the last 2 hours to have the price recorded in storage.

Terms and Conditions Analysis

Detection of abusive clauses and fine print

Automatic + optional AI
📋
T&C and fine print detector + Mini-map
When detecting a terms and conditions page, it scans the text looking for matches against a database of clauses classified into three risk levels. Red level (critical): automatic renewal without notice, class action waiver, forced arbitration, limitation of liability. Orange level (caution): unilateral modification of terms, variable pricing, no-refund policy. When clauses are found, adds a colored border to the affected paragraph and a badge with the clause type. On the right side of the screen a mini-map appears with colored dots that allow jumping directly to each flagged clause. The popup shows the total count with an "Explain in plain language" button that uses AI.
✓ Zero cost — local detection Optional AI — ~€0.002/query
Where to test it

Domestika (domestika.org/es/terms) — has forced arbitration clauses, automatic renewal, and class action waivers. The result is particularly striking. Also works on Amazon Prime, Netflix, and any subscription service.

Hotel California + Forced Continuity
Extension of the T&C detector specialized in detecting clauses that make it difficult or impossible to cancel a service: "to cancel call by phone", "cancellation via certified letter", "written notice", "in person", and abusive notice periods of 30/60/90 days. When detected, marks the paragraph and adds a specific badge ("📞 You must call to cancel") with a "Copy" button that generates the complaint text. Also detects "will renew automatically" and "at the end of the free trial" (Forced Continuity).
✓ Zero cost — local regex
Where to test it

Any online gym, subscription platform, or service with a phone cancellation clause. Domestika Plus terms trigger this feature.

AI returns policy detector
On product pages, searches for returns policy text using semantic selectors and text regex. If it finds a policy with more than 30 words or trap conditions ("does not apply", "store credit only", "sale items excluded"), it sends it to Claude, which returns a discreet toast in the bottom-right corner with three clear lines: whether the return is free or has a cost, how many days you have, and whether the refund is in cash or store credit. The toast disappears after 25 seconds.
AI — ~€0.002/query

Main panel

Features accessible by opening the Kibbo popup in the browser toolbar

Requires popup
🌙
Zenith Mode
Physically removes from the DOM all psychological pressure elements injected by the store: urgency banners, countdowns, elements with classes associated with purchase pressure. Unlike the ON/OFF toggle (which hides Kibbo's own elements), Zenith Mode cleans the page of the store's own elements. Useful for distraction-free browsing on stores like Temu or AliExpress with a high density of manipulation elements.
✓ Zero cost — local DOM manipulation
👻
Ghost Mode
Removes tracking cookies from the current domain and cleans tracking parameters from the URL (utm_source, utm_medium, gclid, fbclid, aff_id, and 20+ more). After cleaning, reloads the page so the store treats the user as a new visitor, which in some cases allows seeing prices without dynamic personalization. Includes a prior warning that it may log you out on some websites.
✓ Zero cost — local cookie and URL manipulation
🤔
Anti-impulse reflection pause
When pressed, launches an overlay screen that covers the entire page for a few seconds with a reflective question and phrases designed to interrupt impulse buying. The phrases rotate showing real statistics about impulse purchases and their impact. After the pause the user can close or continue. Useful as a self-control tool during shopping sessions.
✓ Zero cost — local overlay
📊
Store ranking
Cumulative history of all websites where Kibbo detected traps, ordered by number of detections. Shows the domain, a color risk bar (red/orange/yellow/green), and the total count. Updated automatically on each visit. Allows the user to see which stores are most aggressive with their dark patterns over time.
✓ Zero cost — local storage
🔏
Certified Snapshot
Generates a downloadable HTML document with validity as documentary evidence. Collects: full URL, domain, page title, detected price, readable date and time, ISO 8601 timestamp, time zone, browser and version, operating system, public IP address (obtained via api.ipify.org). Calculates a SHA-256 hash over the page content plus all metadata. The serial number KBB-YYYYMMDD-XXXXXX is derived from the hash itself, making each capture unique and unrepeatable. The document can be submitted to OMIC, OCU, FACUA, or as documentary evidence.
✓ Zero cost — local WebCrypto API cryptography + free external NTP
Cryptographic integrity

Time stamping uses the worldtimeapi.org API (with fallback to timeapi.io) to obtain a verified NTP timestamp, independent of the device's clock. The HMAC-SHA256 signature and content hash are calculated locally in the browser's service worker using the native WebCrypto API.

Review verifier
Automatically extracts the rating distribution (1★ to 5★) from compatible product pages and shows a bar chart in the popup. Detects unusual statistical patterns such as extreme polarization (many 1★ and 5★, few in between) or discrepancy between a high average and a high percentage of 1★. Statistical analysis is local and free.

Toolkit — Advanced tools

Panel tab with on-demand analysis

Includes AI features
🔍
Smart price comparator
On product pages, Kibbo detects the product category (electronics, fashion, sport, home, books, etc.) and shows a list of alternative stores grouped by sector. The logic doesn't always show all stores: if you're looking at sports shoes, it suggests Decathlon, Sprinter, and JD Sports — not Amazon Fresh. For electronics, it suggests PCComponentes, MediaMarkt, and Fnac. The links open a product search in each alternative store with the product name as the query. Stores marked as "Recommended option" are those with a known returns policy and good reputation in the sector. The buyer can activate affiliate links in the recommended stores — each link can include its own affiliate tag, turning the comparator into a recurring revenue source for each purchase the user makes following a Kibbo recommendation.
✓ Zero cost — local categorization logic
Categorization logic

Kibbo identifies the product category by analyzing: the current domain (if Adidas → sport category), the URL path (if it contains /ropa/, /electronics/), the page title, and the navigation breadcrumbs. With that category it selects the most relevant group of alternative stores from a table of 12 predefined categories.

🎣
AI phishing analyzer
A text box where the user pastes a suspicious SMS or email. Claude analyzes the message and returns in 2-3 seconds a clear verdict (PHISHING / LEGITIMATE / SUSPICIOUS) with confidence level (High/Medium/Low), the signals found, and an action recommendation. The analyzer correctly distinguishes between legitimate messages from real entities (official domain, no data request) and phishing (fake domain, urgency, request for payments or credentials).
AI — ~€0.001/query
Examples to test

Phishing: "CORREOS: Your package is held for €1.79 in customs fees. Pay at: correos-envio-pago.com/ES2847163" — should detect it as PHISHING.
Legitimate: "CORREOS: Your shipment is on its way. Track at correos.es or call 900 400 500" — should return LEGITIMATE.

Explain T&C in plain language (AI)
When the T&C detector has found clauses on a page, the "Explain in plain language" button appears in the popup. When clicked, it sends the detected clauses to Claude, which returns a plain-language summary without legal jargon: what each clause implies, what rights it affects, and what the user should do if they disagree. The result appears directly in the popup.

Settings and control

Buttons and settings for Kibbo's behavior

Control
⚡
Hero ON/OFF button
Prominent button in the popup header that activates or pauses Kibbo completely across all tabs. When in PAUSED state, removes from the DOM all elements injected by Kibbo (pills, badges, banners) and sends the signal to the active tab's content script to stop scanning. The state is persisted in chrome.storage.local to maintain it between sessions.
✓ Zero cost
🚦
Visual traffic light on the icon
The Kibbo icon badge in the browser toolbar changes color according to the risk level detected on the current page: green (no traps), yellow with "!" (minor traps detected), red with "!!" (serious traps or domain on the blacklist). Resets automatically when navigating to a new page.
✓ Zero cost
🚫
Domain whitelist
From the Toolkit or the right-click context menu ("Disable alerts here"), the user can add any domain to their personal whitelist. On future visits to that domain, Kibbo will not run any analysis. The whitelist is saved in chrome.storage.local and is reversible from the same button.
✓ Zero cost
🤖
Automatic internal whitelist
Kibbo includes a hardcoded whitelist of more than 100 types of websites where it doesn't make sense for it to act: social networks (Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok), email (Gmail, Outlook), online banking (BBVA, Santander, CaixaBank, ING), Spanish public administration (sede.gob.es, aeat.es, sepe.es, dgt.es), European institutions, productivity platforms (Google Docs, Notion, Slack), streaming (Netflix, Spotify), and more. On these sites Kibbo does not run any code.
✓ Zero cost
👋
Onboarding
When installing the extension for the first time, a 4-slide welcome screen appears explaining the traffic light, the main features, and the privacy policy. It is only shown the first time thanks to a flag in chrome.storage.local. Designed with Kibbo's branding (Oxanium + DM Sans, cyan-purple gradient).
✓ Zero cost
📅
Monthly statistics
The Monitor panel shows how many traps Kibbo detected that month and calculates a user experience level (Novice → Expert → Expert Level) based on the cumulative history. Statistics are calculated locally from storage without sending data to any server.

Technical architecture

Stack, privacy, and operational costs

⚙️
Technical stack
Chrome extension Manifest V3. Content script (content.js ~6,500 lines), popup (popup.html + kibbo.js ~2,000 lines), service worker (background.js). No framework — pure vanilla JS to minimize size and maximize compatibility. Backend: Cloudflare Worker (API proxy) + custom domain. No database. No proprietary server. All user state lives in chrome.storage.local.

Privacy

Kibbo does not collect any browsing data. It does not send visited URLs to any server. The price history, whitelist, store ranking, and all preferences live exclusively in the user's browser's chrome.storage.local. AI calls go through the Cloudflare Workers proxy that acts as an intermediary — the analyzed text never includes the user's personal data, only the public content of the page or the message the user pastes manually.
