The problem

Seven extensions is seven trust relationships

Running seven separate security extensions creates seven separate background processes, seven sets of content scripts injected into every page you load, and seven separate trust relationships with seven different developers — any of whom could be acquired by a data broker tomorrow.

The EFF has documented the pattern: legitimate extensions accumulate users, get acquired, and the new owner has read access to everything those users browse. CISA has flagged maliciously updated extensions as a documented supply chain attack vector.

More extensions doesn't mean more protection. It means more attack surface.

How it works

Step by step

  4. One content script per page instead of seven. One runtime instead of seven competing processes.
  5. The source is open — you can verify exactly what data is read and confirm nothing leaves your browser that shouldn't.
